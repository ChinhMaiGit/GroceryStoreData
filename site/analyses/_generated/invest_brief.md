He: I'm 49. I spent 26 years working for Solvik Handel before deciding to open my own place. I had about 60,000 euros saved, and I took a spot on Granstigen, in Vidnes. We opened in 2025.

He: A full grocery, small format — about 128 products on the shelf at any time. People come because we're close and we have what they need — that's the whole business. I'd guess close to 58 percent of what we take is by card, the rest cash.

**Q: Walk me through the routine.**

He: Same routine most weeks. Deliveries come every Wednesday, one truck. I do the shelf prices myself, only when they've genuinely drifted.

**Q: And 2025?**

He: A quiet year. We took in about 676,000 euros.

**Q: And 2026?**

He: A quiet year. We took in about 713,000 euros.

**Q: And 2027?**

He: My lease renewed and the rent went up 12 percent, from about 960 euros to about 1,100 euros a month. We took in about 728,000 euros that year.

**Q: What do you think happened?**

He: Honestly, I don't have a complaint. Business is good, and now I have to decide what to do with what I've saved — put it back into the shop, or just leave it be. That's the actual question.

**Q: Anything I should know about the records before you start?**

He: Yes, be careful with 4 things. First, the till terminal mis-rings an item now and then — I void it and re-ring it, so you'll find 280 paired correction lines in the receipts over the whole period, nothing missing, just messy. Second, my supplier's system has posted the same invoice twice before — I caught 65 such duplicates myself, but I'll admit I don't check every single one. Third, some delivery paperwork never got typed in — there were weeks I was alone and exhausted, and I paid the supplier for goods you won't find an invoice line for; my bank-account ledger is still right, I just can't always show you the paper behind it. Fourth, the little weather log I keep goes dark for a few days now and then — 17 missing day(s) over the whole period — the station on the roof is nothing fancy.

**Q: What does a useful answer look like to you?**

He: Pages I can read. A number, and how sure you are behind it. Don't be polite about it either.
